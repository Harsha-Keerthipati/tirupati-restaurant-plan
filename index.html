05 — Operations

The standardisation system

No dedicated chef. A bulletproof system where any trained helper executes every dish to the same standard — every single day.

01

Saturday Masala Batch

Every Saturday, 7-day batches of sambar powder, rasam powder, chicken/mutton masala, chutney bases and Kesari mix are prepared, labelled and stored airtight. The kitchen never improvises from scratch.

02

Laminated Recipe Cards

Every station has a laminated card with exact oil, masala quantities, cook time, flame level and a photo of the finished dish. No guesswork. No variation. Same result every time.

03

Pre-Service Taste Check

Before every lunch service, aunt or cousin tastes sambar, rasam and one curry. Fixed before customers arrive — not after complaints roll in.

04

Video Recipe Archive

Aunt cooks every dish on video, stored on Google Drive. Recipe insurance — if she’s unwell, the kitchen still knows exactly what to do.

05

Portion Control Tools

Fixed-size ladles for every curry. Standard scoop for rice. At 400+ covers a day, ₹5 over-portioning per plate = ₹2,000/day lost = ₹52,000/month. Tools are profit protection.

06

Daily Stock Register

Every morning cousin logs raw material stock. Every evening, logs consumption. Catches theft, waste and miscalculation before they become problems.

Sunday Fish Curry — The Weekly Event

Limited plates. Fresh fish bought Saturday evening. Announced on WhatsApp every Saturday at 7pm. Priced at ₹160–180. Builds a Pavlovian weekly habit in regulars. 4 Sundays = ₹24,000–36,000 bonus revenue per month.

06 — Growth

From 20 seats to a scalable business

The real constraint is kitchen speed, not seating. With standardised masalas and pre-prepped ingredients, the kitchen can serve 500+ covers a day across all channels.

🡳
Dine-in
20 seats × 6–8 turns per session. South Indian meals are fast — 8–12 min breakfast, 15–20 min lunch. 120–160 covers per session.
📦
Parcel Counter
Dedicated counter, one helper. 50–80 parcel orders during peak. No seat needed. Pure throughput revenue in parallel with dine-in.
🛵
Swiggy / Zomato
From month 2. Adds ₹1–2L/month with no seating cost. Watch commission — keep delivery mix balanced with dine-in.
🍱
Tiffin Subscriptions
From month 3. ₹1,500/month per subscriber. 30 subscribers = ₹45,000/month guaranteed, predictable income.
